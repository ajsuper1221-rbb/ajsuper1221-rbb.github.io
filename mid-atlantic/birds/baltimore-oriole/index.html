Baltimore Oriole

Bright orange and black, famous for its hanging nest and musical song.

How to spot one

Look for a slender songbird with bright orange underparts and a black head and back. Males are especially vivid, while females are more yellow-orange with softer markings.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Baltimore Orioles prefer open woodlands, tall shade trees, parks, and river edges. They’re often seen high in the canopy rather than at ground level.

What they eat

  • Insects (especially caterpillars)
  • Fruit (oranges, berries)
  • Nectar

Listen for this

Orioles have a clear, flute-like song made up of rich whistles. Their calls often sound slower and more musical than many backyard birds.

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot the bright orange belly?
  • Do you see the black hood on the head?
  • Can you find a hanging nest high in a tree?
