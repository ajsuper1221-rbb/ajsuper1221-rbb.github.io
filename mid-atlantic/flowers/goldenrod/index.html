Goldenrod

A bright yellow sign that summer is ending and fall is near.

How to recognize it

Goldenrod plants are tall and upright with many small yellow flowers grouped together along arching stems or plume-like clusters. The flowers are tiny but packed closely, creating a bold yellow display.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Goldenrod in fields, meadows, roadsides, and sunny open spaces. It often grows in large patches and stands out when many other flowers have faded.

Why it’s important

  • Late-season nectar for bees and butterflies
  • Supports dozens of insect species
  • Provides food for birds through seeds and insects

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot the tiny flowers that make up the big cluster?
  • Watch for insects—how many different ones visit?
  • Does Goldenrod grow alone or in a big group?
