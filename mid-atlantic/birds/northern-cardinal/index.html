Northern Cardinal

A backyard classic: bright red, crest up, and hard to miss.

Tip: this photo works great as a “field guide” image because the bird is clear and the background is simple. (Add a photo credit here if you have one.)

How to spot one

Look for a chunky songbird with a tall crest (that pointed “hairdo”) and a thick orange beak. Adult males are famous for their bright red color and a black face mask around the beak.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Cardinals love shrubs, hedges, parks, and backyards—especially places with cover. In many Mid-Atlantic neighborhoods, you can spot them all year.

What they eat

  • Seeds (sunflower is a favorite at feeders)
  • Berries
  • Insects (especially in warmer months)

Listen for this

Cardinals often sing loud, clear whistles. If you hear a bright, repeated whistle from a tree line, look for a flash of red.

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the crest?
  • Can you spot the black mask around the beak?
  • How many shades of red can you count in the feathers?
