Eastern Redbud

A springtime tree that blooms bright pink before its leaves appear.

How to recognize it

Eastern Redbud is a small tree with bright pink to purple flowers that bloom directly on the branches and trunk—a trait called cauliflory. Later in spring, it grows distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for redbuds along woodland edges, in parks, and planted in yards and neighborhoods. They thrive in partial sun and well-drained soil.

What’s special about it

  • Early bloomer: flowers appear before leaves
  • Heart-shaped leaves in late spring and summer
  • Pollinator friendly: important early nectar source

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot flowers growing right on the branches?
  • Look later in spring—can you find a heart-shaped leaf?
  • Count how many branches have flowers on them.
