Mallard

A classic duck of ponds and parks, with bold colors and a familiar quack.

How to spot one

Mallards are medium-sized ducks. Males have a shiny green head, a yellow bill, and a gray body. Females are mottled brown, which helps them blend in. Both have a blue wing patch called a speculum.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Mallards on ponds, lakes, rivers, wetlands, and even city parks. They’re very adaptable and comfortable around people.

What they eat

  • Aquatic plants
  • Seeds and grains
  • Insects
  • Small aquatic animals

Listen for this

Female Mallards make the classic “quack” sound. Males are quieter, often making softer raspy calls.

Kid challenge

  • Can you tell the male from the female?
  • Look for the blue wing patch when they flap.
  • Count how many ducks are swimming together.
