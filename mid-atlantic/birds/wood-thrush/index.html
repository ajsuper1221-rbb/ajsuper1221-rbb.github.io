Wood Thrush

A spotted forest songster with one of the most beautiful voices in the woods.

How to spot one

Look for a medium-sized thrush with a warm brown back and a bright white chest covered in bold dark spots. The spots are round and easy to see—like someone dotted the chest with ink.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Wood Thrushes live in deciduous forests with a leafy understory, often near streams or moist areas. They prefer shady woods over open fields and are most often seen in the lower canopy or on the forest floor.

What they eat

  • Insects (beetles, ants, caterpillars)
  • Worms and other small invertebrates
  • Berries and fruit (especially later in summer)

Listen for this

The Wood Thrush’s song is flute-like and often sounds like two notes at once. It echoes through the forest, especially at dawn and dusk.

Kid challenge

  • Can you count the spots on the chest (or estimate how many there are)?
  • Listen quietly—can you hear a song that sounds like it’s coming from deep in the woods?
  • Can you spot a thrush hopping on the forest floor looking for food?
