Eastern Bluebird

A cheerful bird with bright blue feathers and a warm orange chest.

How to spot one

Look for a small thrush with bright blue upperparts and a rusty orange chest. Bluebirds often perch on wires, fences, or low branches while watching the ground for insects.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Eastern Bluebirds prefer open fields, meadows, parks, golf courses, and areas with scattered trees. Nest boxes have helped them make a big comeback.

What they eat

  • Insects (beetles, caterpillars, grasshoppers)
  • Worms
  • Berries (especially in fall and winter)

Listen for this

Their call is a soft, musical “cheer, cheerful charmer.” Songs are gentle and pleasant, not loud or harsh.

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot the blue back and orange chest?
  • Count how many times the bird drops to the ground and flies back up.
  • Can you find a nest box nearby?
