Black-eyed Susan

Sunshine-yellow petals with a dark “button” center—an easy summer favorite.

How to recognize it

Black-eyed Susans have bright yellow (sometimes orange-yellow) petals surrounding a dark brown or black center that looks like a little dome. The petals often look slightly “ruffled” or uneven—like sunshine rays.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look in meadows, open fields, roadsides, sunny gardens, and parks. They love sun and often grow in cheerful groups.

Why pollinators love it

  • Bees visit for pollen and nectar
  • Butterflies stop for nectar
  • Seed-eating birds may visit later in the season

Kid challenge

  • Count the petals—how many do you see on one flower?
  • Watch for a bee—does it land on the petals or the dark center?
  • Can you find more than one flower that looks a little different?
