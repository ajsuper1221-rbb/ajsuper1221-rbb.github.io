Flowering Dogwood

A springtime favorite with “flowers” that are really fancy leaves.

How to recognize it

Flowering Dogwood is a small tree with showy “blooms” in spring. The white or pink parts most people call petals are actually bracts—special leaves that surround the tiny true flowers in the center.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for dogwoods along woodland edges, in parks, and in neighborhood yards. They like partial shade and are often found under taller trees.

What’s special about it

  • Spring: white or pink bracts and small clustered flowers
  • Summer: green leaves and developing fruit
  • Fall: bright red berries and reddish-purple leaves
  • Winter: interesting branching shape

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the tiny real flowers in the center?
  • Count the bracts—do you usually see four?
  • In fall, can you spot the red berries?
