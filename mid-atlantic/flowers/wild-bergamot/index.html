Wild Bergamot (Bee Balm)

A wild, spiky purple flower that hums with bees in summer.

How to recognize it

Wild Bergamot has shaggy, spiky-looking purple flowers made up of many thin petals that look a bit like fireworks or wild hair. The plant has square stems and leaves that smell minty when crushed.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look in meadows, open fields, roadsides, and sunny garden edges. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil.

Why pollinators love it

  • Bees visit constantly for nectar
  • Butterflies are drawn to the bright purple flowers
  • Hummingbirds may also stop by

Kid challenge
