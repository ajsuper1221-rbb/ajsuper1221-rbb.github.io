Purple Coneflower

A summer wildflower with drooping petals and a spiky, raised center cone.

How to recognize it

Purple Coneflowers have pink to purple petals that often droop downward, and a tall, spiky center that looks like a cone. The cone is usually orange-brown and feels bristly.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

You’ll see coneflowers in sunny gardens, meadows, and open fields. They’re popular in native plant gardens because they handle heat and dry spells well.

Why pollinators love it

  • Bees and butterflies visit for nectar
  • Later, seed-eating birds may visit the dried flower heads
  • Blooms for a long time in summer

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the spiky cone in the center?
  • Count how many petals the flower has.
  • Watch for a butterfly—does it land on the petals or the cone?
