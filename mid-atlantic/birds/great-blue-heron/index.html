Great Blue Heron

A tall, patient fisherman with a long neck and dagger-like bill.

How to spot one

Great Blue Herons are very tall birds with long legs, a long S-shaped neck, and a sharp, pointed bill. Their feathers are blue-gray, and they often stand perfectly still while hunting.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look along rivers, ponds, lakes, marshes, and shorelines. They’re common around wetlands and can even be seen in suburban areas near water.

What they eat

  • Fish
  • Frogs
  • Crayfish
  • Small mammals (occasionally)

Listen for this

Great Blue Herons are usually quiet, but when they call, it’s a loud, harsh “fraaank” or “kraawk” sound—often heard when flying.

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot a bird that looks statue-still near the water?
  • Watch the neck—does it stay curved until the heron strikes?
  • Count how many steps it takes before stopping again.
