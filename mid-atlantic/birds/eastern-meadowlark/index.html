Eastern Meadowlark

A grassland singer with a bright yellow chest and a beautiful, whistled song.

How to spot one

Look for a medium-sized bird with a bright yellow chest marked by a bold black “V”. The back is brown and streaked, helping it blend in with grasses. Meadowlarks often walk on the ground rather than hopping like many songbirds.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Eastern Meadowlarks live in open fields, meadows, pastures, and grasslands. You’ll often hear them singing from fence posts or see them rising suddenly from tall grass.

What they eat

  • Insects (beetles, grasshoppers, caterpillars)
  • Seeds and grains
  • Spiders

Listen for this

The Eastern Meadowlark is famous for its clear, flute-like whistle. The song often sounds slow and musical, carrying far across open fields.

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the black V on the yellow chest?
  • Can you spot a meadowlark walking through grass instead of flying?
  • Close your eyes—can you tell where the song is coming from?
