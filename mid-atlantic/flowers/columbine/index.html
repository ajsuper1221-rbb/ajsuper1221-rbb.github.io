Columbine

A delicate spring flower with “rocket-shaped” petals and long nectar spurs.

How to recognize it

Columbine flowers often nod downward and have a unique shape: the petals form a star-like bloom with long, curved tubes called nectar spurs stretching out behind them. Many columbines are red and yellow, but some can be blue, purple, or white depending on the species.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for columbine in woodland edges, shady rock gardens, and along trails where the soil drains well. It often grows in places with partial shade.

Why pollinators love it

  • Hummingbirds can reach nectar deep inside the spurs
  • Bees may visit for nectar and pollen
  • Blooms in spring when nectar can be harder to find

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the long spurs behind the flower?
  • Count how many points the flower looks like it has—does it feel like a star?
  • Watch quietly—do any hummingbirds visit?
