Carolina Wren

Small, loud, and full of energy—with a song much bigger than its size.

How to spot one

Carolina Wrens are small, round birds with warm brown feathers, a long curved beak, and a very noticeable white eyebrow stripe above the eye. They often hold their tails upright.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look in yards, gardens, wooded edges, parks, and around brush piles or sheds. They’re curious birds and often live close to people.

What they eat

  • Insects (beetles, spiders, caterpillars)
  • Snails
  • Seeds (occasionally)

Listen for this

The Carolina Wren has a loud, rolling song often described as “tea-kettle, tea-kettle, tea-kettle!” It’s one of the most powerful songs for such a small bird.

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the white eyebrow stripe?
  • Listen for a loud song—does it sound bigger than the bird?
  • Watch how the wren hops and explores tight spaces.
