Blue Jay

A smart, noisy bird with bright blue feathers and a bold crest.

How to spot one

Blue Jays are medium-sized birds with bright blue wings and back, a white chest, and a black necklace across the throat. They have a noticeable crest on their head that rises and falls.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Blue Jays in woodlands, parks, neighborhoods, and forest edges. They’re common at feeders and often travel in family groups.

What they eat

  • Acorns and nuts (a favorite)
  • Seeds
  • Insects
  • Occasional eggs or nestlings

Listen for this

Blue Jays are loud and expressive. Their most common call is a sharp “jay! jay!” They can also imitate hawk calls, which sometimes scares other birds away.

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot the blue-and-black patterns on the wings?
  • Watch the crest—does it go up when the bird is excited?
  • Listen carefully—can you tell a real jay call from a fake hawk call?
