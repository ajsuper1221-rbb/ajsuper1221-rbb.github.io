Downy Woodpecker

A tiny, friendly woodpecker with a bold black-and-white pattern.

How to spot one

Downy Woodpeckers are small and compact with a short, stubby bill. They have a striking black-and-white pattern on their wings and back. Males have a small red patch on the back of the head.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

These woodpeckers are common in woodlands, parks, gardens, and backyards. They’re comfortable around people and often visit bird feeders.

What they eat

  • Insects (especially beetles and larvae)
  • Seeds
  • Suet (a favorite at feeders)

Listen for this

Their call is a sharp “pik!” sound. You may also hear a short, rapid drumming as they tap on trees.

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the black-and-white stripes?
  • Do you see a tiny red spot on the head?
  • Listen for tapping—can you find where it’s coming from?
