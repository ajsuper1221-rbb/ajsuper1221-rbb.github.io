Ruffed Grouse

A forest “hidden bird” with amazing camouflage—and a springtime drumming sound.

How to spot one

Ruffed Grouse are medium-sized, chicken-like birds that blend into the woods with mottled brown, gray, and tan feathers. Look for a rounded body, a small head, and a fan-shaped tail (often with a darker band near the tip).

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

They live in forests with thick undergrowth—especially young woods, woodland edges, and mixed forests. You’re more likely to spot one on or near the ground than perched out in the open.

What they eat

  • Buds and twigs (especially in winter)
  • Leaves and berries
  • Insects (more common in warmer months)

Listen for this

In spring, males make a famous “drumming” sound by beating their wings. It starts slow and speeds up—like a soft engine starting or a distant thump-thump-thump.

Kid challenge

  • Can you find a place where a grouse could hide (leaf litter, shrubs, fallen logs)?
  • How many “wood colors” can you name in its camouflage?
  • If you hear the drumming sound, can you point to where it’s coming from?
