Common Milkweed

A powerhouse native plant that helps monarch butterflies survive.

How to recognize it

Common Milkweed is a tall plant with broad, oval leaves and large, round clusters of pink to purple flowers. If a leaf or stem breaks, it releases a milky white sap.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Common Milkweed in fields, meadows, roadsides, and sunny open areas. It thrives in disturbed soils and open spaces.

Why it’s so important

  • Monarch caterpillars eat only milkweed leaves
  • Butterflies and bees visit the flowers for nectar
  • Seed pods spread silky fluff that carries seeds on the wind

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the round flower ball?
  • Spot an insect—does it look like a butterfly, bee, or beetle?
  • Later in the season, can you find a seed pod?
