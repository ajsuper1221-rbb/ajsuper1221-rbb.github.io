Sunflower

A big, bright “sun” on a stem—loved by pollinators and seed-eating birds.

How to recognize it

Sunflowers are tall plants with a thick stem, large rough leaves, and a big flower head. What looks like one giant flower is actually made of many tiny flowers packed together in the center, surrounded by bright yellow petals.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

You’ll see sunflowers in gardens, farms, fields, and sunny edges of open spaces. Some species are wild, while others are grown on purpose for their seeds.

Why animals love it

  • Bees and butterflies visit the flower center for nectar and pollen
  • Birds love the seeds later in the season
  • Other wildlife may eat fallen seeds

Kid challenge

  • Count petals—how many “sun rays” do you see?
  • Look at the center—can you see tiny patterns where seeds will form?
  • Watch the flower over time—does it look different as seeds develop?
