American Goldfinch

A tiny, bright songbird that loves seeds—and sounds like it’s saying “po-ta-to-chip!”

How to spot one

In summer, male goldfinches are easy to recognize: bright yellow body, black wings with white markings, and a small cone-shaped beak. Females and winter birds are more muted—often olive or pale yellow.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look in open fields, meadows, parks, gardens, and along roadsides where wildflowers grow. They also visit feeders—especially if you offer seeds.

What they eat

  • Seeds (especially thistle/nyjer and sunflower)
  • Flower heads (they love pulling seeds from plants)
  • Small insects (occasionally, especially in summer)

Listen for this

Goldfinches have cheerful, bouncy calls. One common flight call is often described as “po-ta-to-chip!” as they bounce through the air.

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the black wings and white wing bars?
  • How many goldfinches can you spot without moving your feet?
  • Find a flower or plant with a seed head—can you imagine a goldfinch eating from it?
