American Robin

A classic sign of spring with a red-orange chest and a happy, ringing song.

How to spot one

American Robins are medium-sized birds with a gray-brown back, a red-orange chest, and a yellow bill. They’re often seen standing upright on lawns, pausing to listen for worms.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for robins in yards, parks, lawns, golf courses, and woodland edges. They’re one of the most common birds in towns and suburbs.

What they eat

  • Worms (a favorite)
  • Insects
  • Berries and fruit

Listen for this

Robins sing a cheerful, ringing song, often heard early in the morning. Their call sounds like a series of clear whistles.

Kid challenge

  • Watch how a robin tilts its head to listen for worms.
  • Can you spot the red-orange chest from far away?
  • Count how many hops it takes before stopping.
