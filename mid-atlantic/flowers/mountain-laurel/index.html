Mountain Laurel

A woodland shrub with candy-like blossoms—beautiful in late spring.

How to recognize it

Mountain Laurel is an evergreen shrub with shiny, leathery leaves and clusters of pink-and-white flowers that look like little cups or bowls. Many flowers have darker pink dots or lines inside, like delicate decorations.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look in woodlands, especially on rocky slopes and along forest edges. It often grows in groups, creating thick, leafy shrubs.

What’s special about it

  • Evergreen: leaves stay green year-round
  • Flower clusters: many blooms packed together
  • Late spring: often blooms after many early wildflowers

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot the dots or lines inside the flowers?
  • Look closely—do the flowers look like tiny cups?
  • Find a leaf—does it feel thick and shiny?
