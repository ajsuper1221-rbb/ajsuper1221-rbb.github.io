Trillium

A quiet woodland favorite built around the number three.

How to recognize it

Trilliums are easy to spot once you know the trick: everything comes in threes. Each plant has three broad leaves, three petals, and three sepals. Flowers are often white, but some species are pink, red, or purple.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for trilliums in shady deciduous forests, especially in rich, undisturbed soil. They bloom early in spring before trees fully leaf out.

What’s special about it

  • Spring ephemeral: blooms briefly, then disappears
  • Slow growing: can take years to flower
  • Forest indicator: often found in healthy woodlands

Kid challenge

  • Can you count the three leaves?
  • Count the three petals—do they match the leaves?
  • How many trilliums can you find without stepping off the trail?
