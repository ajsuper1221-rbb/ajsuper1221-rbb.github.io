Wild Lupine

A tall spike of blue-purple blooms—like a bouquet on a stem.

How to recognize it

Wild Lupine has a tall flower spike covered in many small blooms. The flowers are often blue to purple, sometimes with white highlights. The leaves are distinctive: each one is made of several narrow leaflets that spread out like a hand or star (called palmate leaves).

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Wild Lupine in open sandy areas, sunny woodland edges, pine barrens, and dry meadows. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil.

Why it’s special

  • Native wildflower that supports many pollinators
  • Pea family plant (its flowers have a “pea” shape)
  • Often grows in patches where soil is sandy and sunny

Kid challenge

  • Can you find the leaf that looks like a hand or a star?
  • Count how many tiny flowers are on one spike.
  • Watch for bees—how many visit the flower spike in one minute?
