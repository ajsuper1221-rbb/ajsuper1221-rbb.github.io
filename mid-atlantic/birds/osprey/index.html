Osprey

A powerful fish-hunter known for dramatic dives and huge stick nests.

How to spot one

Ospreys are large birds of prey with long, narrow wings and a white head marked by a dark stripe through the eye. They often hover over water before plunging down to catch fish.

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Ospreys near rivers, bays, lakes, and coastlines. They’re common around the Chesapeake Bay and often nest on platforms, poles, or other high structures near water.

What they eat

  • Fish (almost exclusively)

Listen for this

Ospreys have a high, whistling call that sounds sharp and repeated, often heard near nests.

Kid challenge

  • Watch for a bird hovering over water.
  • Can you spot the dark eye stripe?
  • If it dives, did it come back up with a fish?
