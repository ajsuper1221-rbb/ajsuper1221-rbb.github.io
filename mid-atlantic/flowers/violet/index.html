Violet

A small spring wildflower with heart-shaped leaves and cheerful purple blooms.

How to recognize it

Violets are low-growing plants with heart-shaped leaves and five-petaled flowers that are usually purple, blue, or violet. Some species may appear white or yellow.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for violets in lawns, gardens, woodlands, and along paths. They often pop up in early spring, even before grass has fully greened.

What’s special about it

  • Early bloomer: one of the first flowers of spring
  • Ground cover: spreads gently across open areas
  • Pollinator friendly: visited by bees and butterflies

Kid challenge

  • Can you find a leaf shaped like a heart?
  • Count the petals—do you see five?
  • How many violet flowers can you spot in one small area?
