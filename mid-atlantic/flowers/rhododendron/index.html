Rhododendron

A bold woodland shrub covered in big, colorful spring blooms.

How to recognize it

Rhododendrons are evergreen shrubs with large, leathery leaves and rounded clusters of showy flowers. The blooms can be pink, purple, white, or red, and often appear all at once in spring.

Where you’ll find it (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for rhododendrons in woodlands, especially in cool, moist, shaded areas. Many are also planted in parks and gardens.

What’s special about it

  • Evergreen: leaves stay green year-round
  • Big blooms: flowers appear in large clusters
  • Woodland plant: thrives in shade and cool conditions

Kid challenge

  • Can you find a flower cluster bigger than your hand?
  • Look closely—how many individual flowers are in one cluster?
  • Touch a leaf—does it feel thick and leathery?
