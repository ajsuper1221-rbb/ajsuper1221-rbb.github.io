Red-tailed Hawk

A broad-winged soaring hawk with a famous scream and a rusty-red tail.

How to spot one

Red-tailed Hawks are large birds of prey with broad, rounded wings and a short, wide tail. Adults usually show a rusty-red tail and a pale chest with a darker “belly band.”

Where you’ll see them (Mid-Atlantic)

Look for Red-tailed Hawks in open areas like fields, highways, parks, and forest edges. They often perch on tall trees or poles and soar high in wide circles.

What they eat

  • Small mammals (mice, squirrels, rabbits)
  • Birds
  • Reptiles

Listen for this

The Red-tailed Hawk’s call is a long, raspy scream often heard in movies. It sounds like “keeeeee-ahhh” and is frequently used as a sound effect for many different raptors.

Kid challenge

  • Can you spot the red tail when the hawk flies?
  • Watch it soaring—how wide are its wings?
  • Listen carefully—does the call sound familiar from TV or movies?
